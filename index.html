PUSH



Push Day focuses on all the muscles involved in pushing movements - mainly the chest, shoulders, and triceps. But this particular workout is designed to place maximum emphasis on chest and shoulders, with minimal stress on triceps. The session starts with compound presses like the Incline Dumbbell Press and Flat Machine Press, which build mass and strength in the upper and mid chest. To isolate and carve detail into the chest, Cable Chest Flys and the Pec Deck Machine target the inner and outer pecs with controlled movements. For shoulders, the Seated Dumbbell Shoulder Press serves as the primary compound lift, building front and side delts. Cable Lateral Raises follow to shape and widen the shoulders by hitting the medial delts. Finally, Rear Delt Flys balance out shoulder development and improve posture by targeting the often-neglected rear delts. This 7-exercise push session gives you a complete upper-body push stimulus focused on growth, shape, and symmetry without overwhelming the triceps.



CHEST

  • Incline Dumbbell Press-3 sets of 8-12
  • Flat Machine Press-3 sets of 10-12
  • Cable Chest Fly (Low to High)-3 sets of 12-15
  • Pec Deck Fly (Butterfly Machine)-2 sets of 15


SHOULDERS

  • Seated Dumbbell Shoulder Press - 3 sets of 8-12
  • Cable Lateral Raises (One Arm or Both)- 3 sets of 12-20
  • Rear Delt Fly (Reverse Pec Deck or Dumbbell)- 2-3 sets of 15

Back day focuses on strengthening and building the muscles of your back, which include the lats (latissimus dorsi), traps (trapezius), rhomboids, and spinal erectors. A strong back not only improves posture and performance in other lifts but also gives your physique a wider, more powerful look-especially through the V-taper effect. This day often includes pulling movements like rows and pull-downs, which help build thickness and width. Including abs on back day is a smart move because both muscle groups dont interfere with each other too much. While your back workouts are pull-based and require minimal core isolation, adding a few sets of abs helps balance your training and tighten your midsection. Core work can also help stabilize your spine during heavy back exercises like deadlifts or bent-over rows.



BACK

  • Pull-Ups or Lat Pulldown - 3 sets of 8-12
  • Barbell Bent-Over Rows - 3 sets of 8-10
  • Chest Supported Dumbbell Row - 3 sets of 10-12
  • Straight-Arm Lat Pulldown - 3 sets of 12-15
  • Face Pulls or Rear Delt Fly - 3 sets of 12-15


ABs

  • Hanging Knee Raises (Controlled) - 3sets of 15
  • Decline Bench Sit-Ups (Weighted if possible) - 3sets of 12
  • Abs Wheel Rollouts - 3sets of 10


LEGS



Leg day is all about building a strong foundation. Training your legs not only improves lower body strength but also boosts overall balance, coordination, and athletic performance. Key leg exercises like squats, lunges, leg presses, and hamstring curls target major muscle groups such as quads, hamstrings, glutes, and calves. Whether you're aiming for muscle growth, fat loss, or better sports performance, never skip leg day-it's essential for a well-rounded physique and functional strength.

REST



Rest day is not just a break - its a critical part of your fat loss and muscle-building journey. After six days of intense workouts, your muscles, nervous system, and even your mind need time to recover, rebuild, and recharge. Every rep you do during the week causes tiny tears in your muscle fibers, and its during rest - not during training - that your body repairs those fibers, making them stronger and more defined. Taking this day off allows your cortisol (stress hormone) levels to drop, reducing the risk of burnout, overtraining, and hormonal imbalances that can stall fat loss. Rest also gives your joints and connective tissues time to heal, preventing overuse injuries that can creep in when recovery is ignored. But this doesnt mean lying in bed all day - active recovery like walking, stretching, or yoga can improve circulation, reduce soreness, and even help with digestion and stress relief. Staying hydrated, sleeping well, and focusing on quality nutrition (especially protein and anti-inflammatory foods) will enhance recovery. Think of Sunday not as a day "off," but as the foundation for next weeks progress. Its the quiet work your body does behind the scenes - repairing, strengthening, and optimizing your results.
